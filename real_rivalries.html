Biggest Rivalries

Real Madrid

VS

FC Barcelona

  • Games Played:280
  • First encounter:1902
  • Real Madrid's biggest victory:11-0
  • FC Barcelona's biggest victory:7-2

The rivalry between FC Barcelona and Real Madrid, famously called El Clásico, is one of the fiercest and most famous in the history of football. It is not just about two of the most successful clubs in the world competing for titles; it is also deeply rooted in historical, political, and cultural tensions. Barcelona, representing Catalonia, has long been associated with Catalan identity, independence movements, and resistance against central Spanish authority, especially during the Franco era. Real Madrid, on the other hand, is seen by many as symbolizing the Spanish capital and the central government’s power. Over the decades, El Clásico has been home to unforgettable matches, legendary players, and dramatic controversies — from controversial refereeing decisions to shocking transfers, like Luis Figo’s infamous move from Barcelona to Real Madrid in 2000. The games are watched by hundreds of millions of fans worldwide, and the rivalry often shapes the narrative of entire seasons. For both clubs, defeating the other can be as important as winning a trophy, and the passion in the stadiums is matched only by the emotions of fans around the globe.

Real Madrid

VS

Atletico Madrid

  • Games Played:240
  • First encounter:1928
  • Real Madrid's biggest victory:4-1
  • Atletico Madrid's biggest victory:4-0

The rivalry between Real Madrid and Atletico Madrid, known as the Madrid Derby, is one of the most passionate and historic in Spanish football. While the two clubs share the same city, they have long represented different values and fan bases. Real Madrid is often linked with global prestige, star players, and a history of dominance both in Spain and Europe. Atlético Madrid, on the other hand, has traditionally been the club of the working class, known for its fighting spirit, resilience, and loyal fan base. Matches between the two are fiercely contested, whether in La Liga, the Copa del Rey, or on the European stage. The rivalry reached worldwide attention when the sides met in the 2014 and 2016 UEFA Champions League finals — both won by Real Madrid, the second after a dramatic penalty shootout. Under coach Diego Simeone, Atletico has built a reputation for defensive strength, relentless work ethic, and the ability to challenge their wealthier neighbors. For both sets of supporters, the Madrid Derby is about far more than points or trophies — it is about city pride, identity, and the right to claim Madrid as their own. Every match brings passion, tension, and drama, making it one of football’s most compelling rivalries.

Real Madrid

VS

Bayern Munich

  • Games Played:28
  • First encounter:1973
  • Real Madrid's biggest victory:4-0
  • Bayern Munich's biggest victory:4-1

The rivalry between Real Madrid and Bayern Munich is one of the great continental matchups in European football, often referred to as a “Champions League classic.” While not a traditional domestic rivalry, the two clubs have met many times in high-stakes European games, creating a history full of drama, controversy, and unforgettable moments. Bayern is Germany’s most successful club, known for its efficiency, discipline, and attacking strength, while Real Madrid is synonymous with European glory, having won the competition more than any other team. Their encounters have produced legendary matches — from Bayern’s hard-fought wins in the 1970s to Real’s dominant performances in recent years. The ties are often tight and emotionally charged, with memorable goals, red cards, and moments of controversy that have fueled mutual respect and tension. For fans, Real vs Bayern has come to symbolize a clash of footballing cultures: the power and precision of German football against the flair and ambition of the Spanish giants. In the Champions League, few fixtures are as eagerly anticipated, as both clubs see victory over the other as a mark of true European supremacy.
