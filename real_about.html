Brief History

Real Madrid was founded on March 6, 1902, as Madrid Football Club. In 1920, it was granted the title "Real" (meaning "Royal") by King Alfonso XIII. The club quickly became one of the most successful in Spain and was a founding member of La Liga in 1929. Its first league title came in 1932. In the 1950s, under the presidency of Santiago Bernabéu, Real Madrid rose to global prominence. With legendary players like Alfredo Di Stéfano and Ferenc Puskás, they dominated European football, winning the first five European Cups from 1956 to 1960. The 1970s and 1980s saw continued domestic success, as the club focused on building a strong youth system and developing homegrown talent. In the early 2000s, the "Galácticos" era began, marked by the signings of global stars such as Zinedine Zidane, Luis Figo, Ronaldo, and David Beckham. In the 2010s, Real Madrid became dominant in Europe once again. Under manager Zinedine Zidane, they won an unprecedented three consecutive UEFA Champions League titles from 2016 to 2018. In 2022, they secured their 14th Champions League title, further solidifying their reputation as the most successful club in European football history. As of today, Real Madrid holds the record for the most Champions League (15) and La Liga titles (35), making it one of the most decorated and iconic football clubs in the world.

Stadium

  • 🏟️ Name: Santiago Bernabeu Stadium
  • 📍 Location: Madrid, Spain
  • 🛠️ Construction: Started in 1944, opened on December 14, 1947
  • 👤 Named after: Former Real Madrid president – Santiago Bernabeu
  • 👥 Capacity: Around 85,000 seats (after renovation)
  • 🏠 Home to: Real Madrid CF
  • 💰 Renovation: Started in 2019 – modern design, retractable roof, 360° LED screen
  • 🌍 International importance:
    • Hosted Champions League finals (1957, 1969, 1980, 2010)
    • Hosted the 1982 World Cup final ⚽
  • 🛫 Tourist attraction: One of the most visited stadiums in Europe
  • 🎫 Tours: You can visit the museum, locker rooms, VIP boxes, and the pitch
  • 📸 Visual symbol: An icon of Madrid, especially stunning at night ✨
